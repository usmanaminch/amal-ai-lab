Overview

What does the nervous system do?

The nervous system helps the body send messages. It lets you feel, move, think, balance, and respond quickly to danger.

Big ideas

  • Brain: control center that processes information.
  • Spinal cord: main message highway between brain and body.
  • Peripheral nerves: branching pathways that carry messages.
  • Neurons: special cells that send nerve signals.
  • Reflexes: fast protective responses.

Memory line

Sense → Spinal Cord → Brain → Response

Some signals go all the way to the brain. Reflex signals can use a shorter spinal pathway for quick protection.

Cell Body

Axon

A long pathway that carries the nerve signal away from the cell body.

Dendrites

Signal path

How a nerve message travels

This shows a simple path from a body sensation to a body response.

1

Sensory receptor

A receptor notices heat, pressure, pain, or touch.

2

Sensory nerve

A sensory nerve carries the message toward the spinal cord and brain.

3

Spinal cord / brain

The central nervous system processes the information.

4

Motor nerve

A motor nerve carries instructions to a muscle.

5

Muscle response

The muscle moves or reacts based on the message.

Simple version: Receptor → Sensory nerve → Spinal cord / brain → Motor nerve → Muscle

Reflex arc

Why reflexes are fast

A reflex is a quick protective response. It can use the spinal cord before the brain fully thinks about it.

Reflex example

If you touch something hot, sensory nerves quickly carry a warning message to the spinal cord. The spinal cord can send a fast motor signal to muscles to pull your hand away.

  • Sensory nerve: carries information toward the spinal cord.
  • Spinal cord: helps coordinate the fast response.
  • Motor nerve: tells muscles to move.
  • Brain: becomes aware and helps you understand what happened.
