Overview

Why sterile technique matters

Sterile technique means using careful habits to keep germs away from areas, tools, and materials that need to stay clean during medical procedures.

Big ideas

  • Sterile: kept free from germs as much as possible.
  • Non-sterile: not clean enough to touch sterile items.
  • Sterile field: the protected clean area for sterile tools and supplies.
  • PPE: protective equipment like masks, gowns, and gloves.
  • Infection prevention: steps that lower the chance of germs entering the body.

Memory line

Clean hands → PPE → Sterile field → Safe procedure

Real operating rooms use trained teams, strict protocols, and careful monitoring. This project is just a learning model.

Infection prevention

How teams lower risk

This is a high-level learning sequence, not real medical instructions.

1

Clean hands

Hand hygiene helps reduce germs before touching clean supplies.

2

Use PPE

Masks, caps, gowns, and gloves create protective barriers.

3

Protect the field

Sterile tools stay in a protected clean area.

4

Watch boundaries

Teams avoid mixing sterile and non-sterile areas.

5

Speak up

If something becomes unclean, the team communicates and fixes it safely.

Simple version: Clean → Cover → Protect → Watch → Communicate
